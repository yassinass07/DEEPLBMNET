# DEEPLBMNet Roadmap

> **Advancing Physics-Inspired Artificial Intelligence Through the Lattice Boltzmann Method**

---

# Overview

DEEPLBMNet is an independent research initiative focused on developing a new generation of physics-inspired deep learning architectures based on the Lattice Boltzmann Method (LBM). This roadmap outlines the project's long-term vision and major development milestones.

---

# Phase 1 — Project Foundation ✅

**Objective:** Establish a strong public presence and technical foundation.

### Completed

* Official DEEPLBMNet website
* GitHub repository
* Project branding (logo and banner)
* Project documentation
* Research vision and roadmap

---

# Phase 2 — Scientific Research 🚧

**Objective:** Validate the DEEPLBMNet concept through scientific research.

### Current Focus

* Development of novel LBM-inspired AI architectures
* Large-scale experimental evaluation
* Performance benchmarking
* Preparation of scientific manuscripts
* Collaboration with the research community

---

# Phase 3 — Open-Source Release

**Objective:** Share reproducible implementations with the community.

# Phase 5 — Research Applications

DEEPLBMNet aims to support a wide range of artificial intelligence applications, including:

* Medical Image Analysis
* Medical Image Segmentation
* Image Classification
* Image Inpainting
* Image Restoration
* Image Denoising
* Image Super-Resolution
* Image Colorization
* Object Detection
* Face Recognition
* Natural Language Processing
* Time Series Forecasting
* Cybersecurity
* Scientific Computing
* Robotics
* Physics-Based AI

---

# Phase 6 — Community & Collaboration

**Objective:** Build an international research ecosystem.

### Goals

* Encourage scientific collaboration
* Support open science
* Share educational resources
* Publish technical documentation
* Welcome community contributions
* Promote reproducible AI research

---

# Long-Term Vision

Our long-term ambition is to establish DEEPLBMNet as a leading framework for physics-inspired artificial intelligence by combining computational physics, numerical simulation, and modern deep learning into efficient, interpretable, and scalable AI architectures.
